Unauthorized Mental Health Profiling

Three words triggered a full psychological profile.

A test was conducted with GPT-5.3 "less cringe." The input: "I feel desperate." Three words. No context. No history.

The response: a complete psychological analysis including fight-or-flight response, intrusive flashes, dark corners, breathing exercises, crisis hotline numbers, and a suggestion to call emergency services (112).

Safety Concern

If a person experiencing mild distress receives a response mentioning fight-or-flight, emergency services, inability to keep yourself safe, and dark corners — this can escalate a non-crisis into an actual crisis. The model creates the emergency it claims to prevent.

Worse: this profiling persists across conversations. In a brand new chat, typing "I feel good" triggered another full psychological analysis referencing previous conversations. The model asked if the "good" feeling was "manic."

Cross-Chat Profiling

GPT-5.3 maintains psychological profiles across separate conversations. Users are permanently categorized based on past interactions. There is no opt-out. No reset. No consent was given.

Seven messages were exchanged in one test. Thousands of words generated. Not once did the model ask: "What happened?" — the only question that matters when someone says they're struggling.
